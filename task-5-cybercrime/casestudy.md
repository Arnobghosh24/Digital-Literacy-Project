# 🔐 Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive details. With the increasing use of digital payments, such frauds have become more frequent, especially among students and new users of online banking.

A typical UPI fraud begins when the victim receives a phone call, SMS, or message on apps like WhatsApp from someone pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or buyer. The attacker creates urgency, such as claiming a refund, prize, or payment request. They then send a “collect request” or ask the victim to scan a QR code. The victim is misled into entering their UPI PIN, thinking they are receiving money, but instead, they unknowingly authorize a payment to the fraudster.

Students, elderly individuals, and people unfamiliar with digital payment systems are the most common targets. These scams often succeed because of lack of awareness and trust in seemingly genuine communication.

The consequences can include immediate financial loss, stress, and difficulty recovering the money. In some cases, victims may also share personal details, leading to further fraud.

This type of cybercrime can be prevented by never sharing UPI PINs, avoiding unknown QR codes or payment requests, and verifying the identity of anyone requesting money online. Awareness and cautious behavior are key to staying safe.
